Power Shower

IDEA Hacks 2020

I joined my friends to participate in IDEA Hacks, the largest hardware hackathon on the West Coast. I ideated and worked with my teammates to create Power Shower,
an IoT hub that encourages college students to conserve water usage when taking showers in dorms. Power Shower helps students monitor how long they spend in showers
and encourages them to decrease their water consumption using a mobile app. We were fortunate enough to be awarded the runner-ups for the Sustainability category of
the hackathon.

Electric Skateboard from Scratch

January 2019 - May 2019

Electric skateboards are fun but they're rather expensive. In my senior year of high school, I worked with a friend to create a cost-efficient electric skateboard.
We learned to use machine shop tools such as band saws and scroll saws to carve out our own deck from a slab of wood. The skateboard has a single brushless
motor wired to a microcontroller which was able to communicate with a remote controller. The skateboard has a top speed of about 15-20 mph based on the weight
of the rider. The final price of our skateboard was about $300 which is pretty good considering similar reliable products we didn't wholesale purchase our parts
and similar products on the market generally go for above $500.
